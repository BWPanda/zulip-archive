Elisabeth Garbeil (Oct 14 2020 at 14:59):

develCuy said:

Herb Can we then say that Backdrop is sustainable for the low-end market?

@develCuy This post I made several years ago may help you. It goes into detail with all the reasons we switched from Drupal to Backdrop for our mid-level clients. I would add one more reason - the user/developer community is very responsive, far more than Drupal ever was for small agencies, and needed changes get made very quickly (within a few weeks). It makes it a lot more sustainable for us and a much easier transition for our clients who are on Drupal 7 already. The ones we've moved to Wordpress, that are actively updating their sites, don't care for it nearly as much as the ones using Backdrop. It's easier and more intuitive to manage than Wordpress if it's more than just a small blog.
https://www.drupal.org/forum/general/general-discussion/2017-04-27/drupal-8-feedback-from-a-customer-standpoint
