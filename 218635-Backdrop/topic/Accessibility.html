klonos (Feb 26 2021 at 11:28):

I understand the points made above, but wanted to point that ignoring accessibility is considered a form of exclusion. ...having said that, I need to also point out that:
a) there is nothing in our https://backdropcms.org/philosophy page specific to accessibility. We in fact have 2 of our principles mention the "majority", and unfortunate as it may be, the majority does not need accessibility (although as @Phil Ward said, we'd need metrics/data to back that up). If we were to change that, then it would require a PMC decision.
b) even if accessibility is not our priority, I am not sure if it is ethical to be introducing something that we know for sure that it is proven to have known accessibility issues. It is one thing to not be checking features going into core for accessibility, and another to ignore accessibility issues when they are known. It just doesn't feel right.
...also worth noting:

  • Accessibility was a huge + when choosing https://www.smartmenus.org over other 3rd party tools for our OOTB drop-down menus feature.
  • On the other hand, we decided against making the alt text on image fields required. The main reason was that we would cause frustration to sighted users, and quoting @jenlampton form a comment in the issue queue:

    I don't know how to measure frustrated-more-sighted-users vs happy-less-sighted-users. :confused: I think we need more feedback!

...interesting opinions and research facts in both https://github.com/backdrop/backdrop-issues/issues/3006 and https://github.com/backdrop/backdrop-issues/issues/4551 for anyone interested :wink:

indigoxela (Feb 26 2021 at 12:07):

Not sure, which tendencies this discussion has or is supposed to have...

  • I'm convinced that our claim should always be: do it as good as possible. Providing an inaccessible UI - deliberately or by laziness - is not OK
  • If we just didn't realize (which obviously can happen), we should fix it just like any other bug - bad accessibility is a bug IMO

The decision for the given example (img alt attributes not enforced) has possibly happened, because bad (enforced hence potentially silly) alt texts can be worse than no alt text at all. Obviously we sometimes disagree (like with empty alt being better or worse than no alt), but maybe only because we can't determine what's better or worse.
