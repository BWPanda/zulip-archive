klonos (Feb 05 2020 at 15:23):

I joined the Backdrop community in its first days after the fork was announced (amazing how 5 years have gone by!). That time happened to be a very difficult for me in my personal life (moving from Greece to Australia - having to become a long-distance husband and father to 2yo daughter etc.) ...I was trying to keep my sanity by keeping my mind busy with contributions to the community, and trying to delve deeper into webdev and Drupal/Backdrop ...it was really refreshing to join a community that had the "old spark" of the original Drupal community: welcoming novice developers (developer-wannabe's in my case), and not looking down on us for not embracing the new, cool D8 and all its symfony/composer fanciness. I considered the "founding members" to be my friends (not just @jenlampton and @Nate Lampton - talking about the "extended" team of all the people that joined the community early on) ...everyone has been so helpful with all my questions, and they did not seem to mind that I was the "issue-opening bot" on GitHub :sweat_smile: ...back in Drupal-land, I used to be that annoying person in the issue queue, that had all those ideas/suggestions, but most of them were being dismissed, which eventually lead me to be demotivated from contributing ...I couldn't code back then - I was just doing patch testing (no code reviews), brainstorming for solutions to UX/UI problems, providing screenshots/mockups, and helping newcomers "orientate" ...after a while, it was @Geoff St. Pierre (serundeputy) who said to me "You know that you can easily create a pull request by using the GitHub web interface. Right?" ...he went on to show me how to do that, and the rest is history: a PR-creating monster has been created out of me :sweat_smile: ...so a big thank you those that I call "the usual gang" - you know who you are guys and gals ...I really love y'all and consider you my friends :heart:
