StPaulTim (Mar 08 2021 at 22:49):

I was just chatting with @Jack privately about safety precautions during Backdrop LIVE and decided that we should be involving more people in that discussion.

@Jack has written an excellent blog post about self-defense against Zoom bombers. I think that many of the suggestions in this article have wider implications.

https://palantetech.coop/zoomboming-self-defense/tech-guide

I'm particularly interested in the idea of a Safety Team. I envision this as a small group of people that:

1) Are familiar with our code of conduct and understand our expectations for participants.
2) Have access to the tools (such as co-host priviledges in Zoom and facilitator status in the Mural) to intervene if anything happens
3) Are publicly identified as people that should be approached/contacted if anyone has concerns or complaints.

Zoombombing Self-Defense: A Technical Guide
A technical guide for increasing community safety and security when using Zoom Meetings for public or semi-public events.
