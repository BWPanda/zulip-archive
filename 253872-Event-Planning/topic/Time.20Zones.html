I know there's no easy answer to this, but here's a list of each of the event timezones and the number of blocks that have sessions scheduled between 6am and midnight (the times which I figure most people could possibly attend):

  • Oakland, Seattle: 4
  • Chicago, Dallas, Managua: 4
  • New York, Miama: 4
  • Buenos Aires, Brasilia, Santiago: 4
  • Berlin, Oslo, Madrid: 4
  • Johannesburg, Beruit, Athens, Cairo: 4
  • Tokyo, Seoul: 3
  • Sydney: 3

I.e. Americans wanting to attend sessions between 6am and midnight have 4 out of 5 blocks to choose from. Australians on the other hand only have 3.

StPaulTim (Mar 12 2021 at 07:25):

Yes, I understand. There is a reason for this. We advertise Backdrop LIVE as 24 hours. But it's actually 27 hours. We added one additional block, because otherwise Block D would have been the last block during a time slot when most participants are sleeping. We thought it might be good to add one extra block as a final block to wrap up the event at a time when more participants would be available.

This one extra block is least advantageous to Australia and Asia. Which results in the numbers you calculated.

If it has been exactly 24 hours, the opportunities to participate would have been more easily distributed. But the event would have ended at the time of day when we have the least amount of participants.
