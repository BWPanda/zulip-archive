Phil Ward (Feb 18 2021 at 09:14):

I feel like what @Alan Mels is trying to figure out, isn't so much who or what the PMCs are, but rather what they are doing to lead the community. People look to leadership to get things done and find direction. Without that direction and vote of confidence to move one way or another, people sit on their hands, work on their own projects or move on to more interesting things.

Ultimately, Backdrop needs an archetype, a person who is willing to stand up and say: "here is the direction we are going." Someone people can look upto, admire, and be willing to follow. That archetype could be the PMC, it could be a single person. From my observations over the last 6 months, the current active archetype is @jenlampton. She is viewed as a person of reason, respected, respectful and a person people are willing to take direction from. The silent archetype is @quicksketch. When he speaks, people listen. What he says, goes. Both are excellent people for those roles and I would personally love to see them step up and be a little louder.

Many of us want to be passionate and are passionate about Backdrop, but at the same time, completely confused about what there is to be passionate about. Lots of us want Backdrop to succeed. But without the clear path of where it's going and why, it's hard to see any vision of its future.
