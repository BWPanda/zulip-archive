indigoxela (Aug 26 2020 at 12:36):

Hi, @Sev, your situation sounds familiar. I guess, many people are currently hesitant regarding existing D7 web projects and how to proceed.

The extended D7 support (and after that long-term-support) gives people more time to evaluate, but perhaps doesn't make the decision easier. Keep in mind - it may happen that contrib modules your projects rely on run out of support, although D7 core still gets updates.
Also consider that D7 won't get new features or significant enhancements.

Overall, it's some sort of poker. My suggestion would be that you try Backdrop out in a new (smaller) project to figure out if it works for you (I'm confident it will :wink: ).
And when you have enough trust and moving more complex projects to Backdrop looks feasible... then just do it.
