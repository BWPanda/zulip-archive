klonos (Oct 09 2020 at 00:33):

It seems that in Drupal land they've decided to stick with CKEditor and switch to v5: https://www.drupal.org/project/drupal/issues/3091226

klonos (Oct 09 2020 at 00:35):

This seems to be the most important point:

Lack of upgrade path and arbitrary HTML support for content. This is a problem CKSource also shares and is a concern for their customers. They are in the process of hiring people to implement this. The timing is tricky as they plan to complete this later next year, so the timing will be tight for our October 2021 target of inclusion. On the other hand, in this case CKSource is hiring people to implement this, while for the other editors, we would need to figure this out on our own.

klonos (Oct 09 2020 at 00:37):

...also good points:

  • We already have a good working relationship with the CKSource team, our CKEditor 4 integration was implemented with their collaboration and previously agreed on an extension of security support for CKEditor 4 until the end of life of Drupal 8 for the benefit of our users.
  • While a CKEditor 4 to 5 migration does not exist, the same is true for the other editors suggested. While CKEditor 5's stricter document model does come with some challenges for arbitrary HTML, this is also true of other editors suggested with a stricter document model.

klonos (Oct 09 2020 at 00:37):

...and this is an interesting implementation approach: Add optional support for CKEditor 5 in D9 so we can remove CKE 4 from Drupal 10

Herb (Oct 09 2020 at 01:26):
