indigoxela (Jul 07 2020 at 06:53):

Regarding putting functionality in core: the hard part would be choosing the modules. Different sites need different solutions. Some are well-protected with Honeypot alone, some even need combinations of antispam modules, some don't need any additional protection from contrib.

How to figure out what the "80%" are?

klonos (Jul 07 2020 at 09:45):

Usage stats of modules compared to the total amount of Backdrop sites help make decisions... https://backdropcms.org/project/usage/backdrop shows roughly 1250 sites, and https://backdropcms.org/project/usage/honeypot shows 170 ...that's a ~14%
...next, there's the fact that we cannot ship core with a solution that relies on a 3rd party service (like CAPTCHA/reCAPTCHA, Akismet, Molom etc.), as there are privacy/licensing concerns, and also the matter of what happens when these services have outages and/or go out of business.
...another factor is code complexity.
So when it comes to honeypot, it is a simple, self-contained solution ...it may not be as effective for all sites as some of these 3rd party services are, but it is better than no protection at all.
