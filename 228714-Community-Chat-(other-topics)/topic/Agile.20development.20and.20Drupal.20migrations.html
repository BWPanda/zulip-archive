Topic: Agile development and Drupal migrations

{% raw %}

Herb (May 14 2020 at 14:10):

I've been thinking for some time about how Drupal upgrades/migrations push organizations and agencies into un-agile development. I am currently working on a large site migration to Drupal 8 and trying to do it in releasable chunks. But Drupal makes it really hard. It forces a wholesale upgrade, a relatively fixed amount of work, when when the organization is happy enough with their site. I was reading up on migrations and came across this line on Drupalize docs: "This is a great opportunity to give your website a face lift." So they're basically saying, if you're going to have to spent an unknown amount of money on a fixed scope of work, might as well do even more work for more money."

Herb (May 14 2020 at 14:11):

This is the opposite of agile.

Herb (May 14 2020 at 14:12):

Instead we want to make it possible to break the work into smaller chunks where we can release each separately to the public so we can get real world feedback.

Herb (May 14 2020 at 14:12):

But that's really hard to do when you're forced to upgrade everything at once.

Herb (May 14 2020 at 14:14):

In the case of our one client, we're going to have two sites up and running at the same time. All the Drupal 7 functionality that was deeply connected with CiviCRM will stay on the Drupal 7 site until next year. And everything else will go to Drupal 8.

Herb (May 14 2020 at 14:19):

The best case scenario is that the staff and end users will not see much difference in how they use the site. That's a funny way to spend tens of thousands of dollars in the hope of seeing not much change in functionality.

Herb (May 14 2020 at 14:20):

Sure, it'll be more secure and it means they'll get more benefits in the future with new releases. But most of their interaction is with CiviCRM which will be the same.
