Topic: Will Covid-19 delay D7 EOL?

{% raw %}

Herb (Mar 28 2020 at 15:38):

I have zero evidence or insight but I'm wondering if there'll be a move to delay the D7 EOL. Discuss

oadaeh (Jason Flatt) (Mar 28 2020 at 15:44):

I think that the EOL deadline is far enough away that unless the effects of CoVid-19 are 8 or months long, it will not change the EOL. Also, the Drupal Association is currently hurting for money, and I think it's more likely that they will cut resources to D7 to save themselves in the hope that people will move to D8 sooner.

StPaulTim (Mar 28 2020 at 19:22):

My understanding is that the Drupal 7 and 8 are scheduled to reach EOL at the same time. I also understand that the Drupal 8 EOL is somewhat forced by the EOL of Symphony 3.

If Symphony 3 reached EOL on schedule, forcing EOL for Drupal 8, it would seem unlikely (but not impossible) that they would continue to support Drupal 7 after Drupal 8 is no longer supported. However, now that I think about it, if the upgrade from Drupal 8 to Drupal 9 is as easy as they say, then maybe it doesn't matter?

StPaulTim (Mar 28 2020 at 19:23):

I will certainly be MUCH easier for Drupal 8 sites to upgrade to Drupal 9 than it will be for Drupal 7 sites.

klonos (Mar 28 2020 at 21:42):

Dropping support for D8 and leaving D7 on would be admitting that these are two different products. It will be the first time that a version is "skipped" so to say. ...it all depends on numbers I guess. If by Nov 2021 there's still a considerable amount of sites still in D7 (like 100s of thousands), then it would be idiotic to drop support just for the sake of "sticking to the plan".

klonos (Mar 28 2020 at 21:44):

...as @StPaulTim said, dropping support for D8 is being "forced" by the 3rd party software being used (Symfony mainly); whereas the reason for dropping D7 is just policy.
