Hopefully we'll never have to use the CoC to address transphobia in our community, but if we do, the way we deal with the issue will show a lot about how seriously we take that point in there!

jackaponte (Mar 31 2021 at 23:23):

Another important thing we can do is to look for and be aware of issues affecting trans people both in Backdrop and in wider FLOSS (free/libre open source software) communities. There are probably a number of opportunities out there for folks in the Backdrop community to express solidarity with trans people when transphobic stuff is going down in the wider open source world.

jackaponte (Mar 31 2021 at 23:26):

Another basic thing that would go a long way: make it standard for folks to share their pronouns in meetings and other spaces. That way folks aren't assuming people's pronouns and using the wrong ones, PLUS it won't just be trans people (like me) saying their pronouns and everyone else not saying them. Instead of singling trans people out, making it standard for everyone to share pronouns during intros puts the responsibility on everyone to not make assumptions.

jackaponte (Mar 31 2021 at 23:28):

Final thought from my little brainstorm: if we ever do collect gender demographic info about our community, e.g. a Gender field on profiles or surveys, let's make sure it's actually inclusive and respectful! (Male | Female | Transgender | Other as a single-select field doesn't cut it, heh, see https://www.drupal.org/project/drupalorg/issues/1354764 for more on that...)
