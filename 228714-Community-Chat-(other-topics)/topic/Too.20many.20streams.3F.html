StPaulTim (Mar 28 2020 at 01:48):

you dont think too many streams. i'm thinking that.

I think that we should have as few streams as possible, buy I think that there are some good reasons for having some special streams.

1) We have a stream for leadership and PMC, because these are small groups with limited membership. These streams do no effect most users.
2) We have a forum stream for automatic forum notifications, because some people have expressed a preference to not get these messages and the thought was that those who were interested could opt in instead of forcing everyone else to opt out.
3) We have a Community Chat stream, because I think that there should be a place that people feel comfortable chatting about off-topic things. I believe that many users will be uncomfortable bringing up "off-topic" subject in our main stream, especially new users who don't know the customs. For me, having a "community chat" stream gives people explicit permission to bring up other topics and connect as a community beyond the software.
4) We have a code review and contrib stream, I assume because it made sense to someone at some time. I would be happy to remove these channels and wrap that back into the main Backdrop CMS stream. However, I don't mind having them either.
5) Localization stream - makes sense on paper, but I don't know if it's required. I would leave that up to the localization team.
6) Virtual User Group stream - I think we could get rid of that.
7) Core notifications stream - I think that is there for those who are interested in automatic notifications about CORE. It's seems reasonable to keep this in it's own stream, once it's working. For now, that channel is not being used.
8) Dragonbot team - is there to test Dragonbot and chat about issues specific to Dragonbot. But, I'd be happy to get rid of that too. However, I suspect is a narrow group of people that are interested in the Dragonbot.
