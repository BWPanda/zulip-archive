BWPanda (Oct 13 2020 at 10:53):

IMO, the type - * labels in the core issue queue are special in that, I believe, all issues should be labelled with at least of of these. To that end, I've just finished going over the issue queue over the last few days and adding one of those type labels to any issue without one already. I've also made a bookmark for myself of issues without a type label so I can fix future ones from time-to-time. Feel free to use a different type label if I mis-identified any issues...

Herb (Oct 13 2020 at 11:36):

That's helpful. I've changed other people's issue type. Usually when they're really eager for a fix so they make it a bug issue

klonos (Oct 15 2020 at 10:50):

I also try to be setting those as I come across issues without any. I usually find that people may also categorize issues as features when they are tasks, and vice versa.

BWPanda (Oct 15 2020 at 11:03):

Yeah, I've been using 'Feature request' when someone's asking for something to be added/removed/changed but others may disagree or feedback is required to see if there's agreement that's it's the best way forward. And then 'Task' for things on which there's already a general consensus and/or no feedback required.

klonos (Oct 15 2020 at 11:14):

I usually add "feature" to things that are completely new functionality, and when the change is big enough to warrant it being introduced in a minor release. I see "tasks" as something between "features" and bugs. (it's a loophole :sweat_smile:)

BWPanda (Oct 15 2020 at 23:15):

Also, I use 'Task' for Meta issues.
