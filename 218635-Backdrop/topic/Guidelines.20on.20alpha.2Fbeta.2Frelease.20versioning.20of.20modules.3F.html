Topic: Guidelines on alpha/beta/release versioning of modules?

{% raw %}

bugfolder (Feb 19 2021 at 23:11):

I was wondering if there were any Backdrop guidelines for when to call a release alpha or beta or rc versus a full release number?

For the modules I've ported, I gave them all alpha versions to begin with, e.g., 1.x-1.0.alpha1, etc., and marked the releases as "prerelease". I'm starting to wonder when it's time to promote some of them to the next level.

I suspect that for people considering Backdrop and looking for whether their pet contrib modules have been ported, a sea of yellow in the Backdrop Upgrade Status module list might be a bit off-putting. So modules probably shouldn't stay prerelease any longer than appropriate; I'm just wondering what's appropriate?

Any pointers where such guidance is documented would be great. I know that "Tagging for release" on Converting Modules from Drupal discusses the semantic versioning of modules; perhaps a few words about alpha/beta/rc/etc. there might be helpful?

StPaulTim (Feb 19 2021 at 23:39):

BWPanda (Feb 19 2021 at 23:59):

I'd suggest that it's up to the individual maintainer based on what they think is appropriate for that project. I don't think there is/should be any overall requirements for this (I.e. what works for one project might not work for another).

bugfolder (Feb 20 2021 at 00:02):

I wasn't so much looking for _requirements_ as for guidelines/best practices/advice. I'd like to strive for as common an approach as possible.

oadaeh (Jason Flatt) (Feb 20 2021 at 00:21):

I don't think there are any guidelines or best practices on that. As @BWPanda said, it's really up to the maintainer. If you're still in full dev mode, I wouldn't promote up, but once you get it to a point where you're not making a bunch of changes, tag it and wait a few weeks to see if anyone comes up with anything. If nothing major is found, move it up to the next level. There will always be bugs, so waiting for a release with no bugs means the module will never reach 1.0 status.
