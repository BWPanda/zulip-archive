Phil Ward (Nov 07 2020 at 23:23):

@indigoxela heh... I proposed that webp was put into core of D7 not very long after its release. My suggestion was that webp was pushed as the default image with a fallback to the more stable formats. Needless to say, it never went anywhere. Now that D9 is out, there's talk about them implementing it in some form though I believe it's just to add it as a recognized format, not to add any sort of fallback.

The two reasons I believe webp never took off:
1) Internet Explorer
2) Nobody wanted to implement a push first fallback else method for the browsers that didn't support it at the time.

Had the big web players (wordpress etc) done this from the beginning, I personally believe webp would have become the default image format years ago. Firefox wouldn't have waited until 2019 to add it and Safari is still yet to add it. Chrome currently holds around a 60% market share which is going to grow to about 85%-90% with Edge now being built off Chromium and FF having a rather sad and painful death.

I'll digress ;-)

Oh, not trying to suggest anything persay, just throwing out some thoughts on the matter.

Is this going to be designed to convert images to webp on upload through gd, imagemagick etc or will this inclusion simply just allow webp files to be uploaded and served?

The intention is to provide support for uploading and handling webp in core (with gd). Conversion to webp (set webp as default image output type) is supposed to go to contrib - which is currently blocked by the lack of core webp support, but should be possible after the PR has been merged.
