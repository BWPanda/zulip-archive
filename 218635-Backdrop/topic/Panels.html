Phil Ward (Oct 30 2020 at 03:21):

@Elisabeth Garbeil What I want...

Well, what I want is a way to expose pretty much anything that can be exposed within BD, into the layout form. I just tested node/% and I do see that it exposes the individual fields like I would expect. Problem 1 solved.

Problem 2 is that the visibility conditions are extremely basic.

image.png

As you can see, there is no "field" visibility condition. This is the powerful piece that is missing.

See, Panels exposes EVERY FIELD on the node as a visibility condition. This allows me to say "Show this block (panel pane) [if] this field is true. If not, hide it." I have fields on one of my websites where some of them contain data, others do not. I tell the visibility rule to "hide all of these fields that are empty (null)".

Let me give you a real-world on one of my e-commerce sites: I have a price field (ubercart) and a custom availability field (list field). I have a visibility set on the price where "if availability NOT = In-Stock, On-Backorder, Sold-Out -> Hide the price field".

Since there is no exposure in the BD visibility to fields, there is currently no way to accomplish this. The Context module for D7 kinda handled this sort of thing, but I never really used it because its focus was more on block visibility where-as Panels focus was literally on EVERYTHING you wanted to expose to the Panel.

So, as of right now, core does not have a way for me to accomplish what I would need because core isn't exposing the fields as visibility conditions. They're exposed on the layout, just not on the visibility.
