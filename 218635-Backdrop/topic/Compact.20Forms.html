BWPanda (May 17 2020 at 17:20):

As for switch statements, that is how they work, and is why you normally need to add a break; at the end of each (to prevent them following-through to each other). It's also why it works when you do this:

switch (condition) {
  case 1:
  case 2:
  case 3:
    // Do something for cases 1, 2 & 3.
  break;
}
