StPaulTim (Jan 25 2021 at 19:53):

@oadaeh (Jason Flatt) - It means that this is a survey of people that are actively involved in the community. Much the way that decisions made during development meetings are inherently skewed towards the opinions of those who show up at meetings. In my view, it's important that we do keep that in mind and never suggest that the results of this survey are anything more than a snapshot of the opinions of those who in Zulip.

I would love to do a broader survey of the community with much greater outreach to end users. I view this survey as a first step in that direction. I see this as an experiment leading towards a much more representative survey in the future.

mostly features a developer would want will be voted up?

This may be true, but I would not assume it. Many of our most active members in the community do not consider themselves developers and some of the developers are very vocal in their advocacy for issues that are most needed by end users. I've recently heard the suggestion that core development may be skewed against the needs of developers, so personally I'm not overly concerned about this becoming too developer centric or at least any more skewed towards developers than our existing tools are.

Certainly, the results of this survey should not be portrayed as anything more than a experimental snapshot of the opinions of those most active in the Zulip portion of the community - leading towards better and more representative surveys in the future.
