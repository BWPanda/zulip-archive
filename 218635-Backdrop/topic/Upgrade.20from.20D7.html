Elisabeth Garbeil (Sep 02 2020 at 15:15):

@Olaf Grabienski,

We’re typically updating and redesigning the site when we migrate, so mainly I use the same tools you would to develop a new site. That’s one of the reasons we don’t just upgrade. We tend not to use drush or composer because we’re a small shop that’s been around a long time and does more than just Drupal, so I have my own scripts. I tried out drush a long time ago and it wasn’t worth the effort for us. If you want to use one of those, I would recommend composer over drush. It’s less overhead in my experience. I use primarily feeds and some custom coding to migrate data from D6 or D7 to Backdrop. I don’t typically bring over anything but content. I recreate the webforms manually, since there’s usually only one or two. The one migration where there were a lot, we revamped the forms anyway, so I would have had to redo them.

We’re not using D8 or D9 at all. It’s too expensive a footprint for any of our clients with no benefit. Hope that helps!

I’ve run into a couple of wrinkles with feeds, but have gotten lots of help on Zulip, so there haven’t ever been any showstoppers with that. I’m pretty happy with it.

Good luck with your upgrade!
