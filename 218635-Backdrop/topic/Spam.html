Phil Ward (Nov 17 2020 at 20:03):

I haven't had a chance to dig into spam prevention, but wanted to throw my two cents out there.

On Drupal 7, I run two spam prevention modules in tandom and get virtually zero spam.

Botcha
Honeypot

I've been testing both recaptcha v2 and v3 and have found it to be an utter joke for spam prevention.

Botcha does experience a bug with pages that require JavaScript to run on them (not exactly sure what the problem is) but outside of that, I have had 20 or so sites over the years running this combination and get very little spam. Maybe several per month.

I've never had anyone complain about their messages not getting received, or problems with submissions.

If someone ever wants to tackle spam prevention, I would recommend looking at those two modules to see if you can reverse engineer why the combination of them is so effective.

Oh, one complaint from users: they can't register because they autofill and blow through it too quickly. I simply tell them to not use autofill.

oadaeh (Jason Flatt) (Nov 18 2020 at 18:04):

I have a large, busy site I have maintained for several years that used Botcha for a while. Then we ran into a problem that was causing non-spam form submissions to be rejected. After many hours of pouring through the code and the issue queue, I was not able to come up with a reasonable solution to the problem (though I found and fixed many other smaller bugs in the process). Part of the problem is that the module is coded to work around the D7 hook system to use OOP in such a way that the code is convoluted and hard to follow. We ended up limiting its use in favor of Honeypot.
