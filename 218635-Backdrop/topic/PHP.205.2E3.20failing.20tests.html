Phil Ward (Jan 18 2021 at 18:34):

Thanks all. I'll be in that boat to agree to raise it for the reason that it's too much overhead for a small community to have to maintain.

I'll note that from my experience, a lot of hosting environments might still offer it, some even out of the box, but they have gotten a LOT better with offering a way to choose multiple versions of PHP (including the latest) which means for a wide variety of people, changing it is a piece of cake.

The biggest problem is what is run for the control panel. This affects drush, composer and other PHP CLI stuff and isn't possible to change without upgrading the control panel.

When Backdrop was forked 6 years ago, the ability to choose was just starting to roll out from control panels and hosting companies. Now it's pretty common place. HostGator for example was stuck on cPanel v56 about 5 years ago, but they had their own in-house version of choosing PHP (which wasn't introduced to cPanel until around v70 I think).

So one thought before raising this, points back to telemetry. Once that can be implemented it will give a better direction on which versions to support.

I have mixed feelings about this and will say that if it's really a burden for us to maintain some of the earlier versions of PHP 5.x, we should CONSIDER our support level and make adjustments.

I guess I'm not seeing the political gain

The Political gain is that Backdrop CMS has promoted itself (in my opinion) as taking care of the users that were left behind by Drupal 8. If we start promoting policies and/or requirements that are MORE restrictive than those supported by Drupal 7, we MIGHT be sending a counter message (that does not mean that we should never do it).

The users still stuck on servers with PHP 5.3 are probably not going to Drupal 8. There is some risk that we loose any opportunity to bring them over to Backdrop if we make life harder for them than Drupal is.

Having said that, it's an argument that must be balanced against other arguments - including the ongoing costs to our community (as mentioned by @Phil Ward ).

I suspect that we may need to change our support version before 2.0 (Remember Backdrop 2.0 will not come before Jan 2025). But, we may want to keep ourselves in line with Drupal 7 until Drupal 7 is no longer supported (Nov 2022).

I'm undecided on this issue, listening to what others have to say.
