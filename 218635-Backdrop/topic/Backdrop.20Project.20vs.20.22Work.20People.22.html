Rob Feature (Mar 22 2021 at 14:19):

Just something to think about in regards to community: Most people in the meetings and here on Zulip are 'Backdrop People". You work to forward the project, make it cooler, etc. Thats obviously extremely essential and necessary. But there are other people (like me, honestly) who make up most communities like this: People who do this as a job but, frankly, probably wont contribute directly to the project without being hired (by a client) to do so. That means we'll contribute ported modules and generally be supportive of the community, but don't really. have interest in getting directly involved in volunteering to make it what it is. I might suggest that outreaching the people like that is important. They grow the project because it's the best tool, not because they want to be involved in the community for the sake of itself. I'm just sharing this because I feel a bit 'on the outside' in this world because this is my work...not my enjoyment :) I think devs like that are super important to communities, but I think they're often forgotten in outreach efforts. All this just to say: That's a group to remember and maybe think about forming some connection with.

Rob Feature (Mar 22 2021 at 14:21):

I guess what I mean is outreach should probably be to 2 different groups: "Those who want to make our project better" and "Those who will make our project better because it's a tool they use daily". Anyone see where I'm coming from? Or is that just me....? :)
