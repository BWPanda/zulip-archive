cellear (Luke McCormick) (Oct 15 2020 at 04:39):

Some things I learned in today's "Introduction to Backdrop" training:

  • You can schedule content to be published at a specified time in the future
  • It includes better handling of phone numbers that let you dial a phone
  • Backdrop can update itself (!)

While defining content types:

  • You can turn off annoying default options
  • You can access the permissions form directly
  • You can define URL alias patterns
  • You can decide whether to display author and date info
  • You can create a node without a path ("Rabbit Hole") that won't display on its own
  • You can schedule comments to disable after a settable period of time.

More things I didn't know, or maybe forgot:

  • You can have it choose to correct image rotation from EXIF data
  • Date in core!
  • I was apparently impressed with manage display - entity view mode, though I don't know what that means.
  • You can float images in Fields display
  • it's awesome to be able to tell them to lazy-load there.
  • The default hero block is actually quite snazzy!
  • You can log in with your email address _or_ your user name.
  • I didn't realize how easy it is to customize the dashboard; I think that's very powerful!
  • Color scheme rocks!
  • You can drag and drop images into WYSIWYG, and it creates file
  • Responsive content edit form
