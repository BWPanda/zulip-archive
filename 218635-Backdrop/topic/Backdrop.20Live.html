bugfolder (Mar 07 2021 at 16:31):

I just released a new module, User Time Zone Tokens, then went to the Backdrop Live page and saw this line:

Our next Backdrop LIVE is happening on March 12-14 (depending upon where you live).

If I may say so, this is an ideal use case for this module (and it was situations like this that prompted me to write it).

The module lets you create time-zone-aware tokens for dates and times and you can use them anywhere you can use tokens (which includes formatted text, if you use the Token Filter module).

Using User Time Zone Tokens, you could have that say

Our next Backdrop LIVE is happening from March 12, 6:00am PST to March 13, 10:00am PST

and have the date, time, and time zone be correct for the current user—even anonymous users.

So if that looks interesting, please take a look at the module. Suggestions, bug-finds, bug-fixes, and anything else are all welcome!

@Graham Oliver - We had numerous different discussions during Backdrop LIVE, but logistically they were all in one room. I had the sense that participation this time was easier than last time, but I would love additional feedback on that. I think we will get better at explaining it to people next time.

To participate this time, all you had to do was come to the single room we set up (the path never changed) at any of the appointed times. During some time slots, we broke into breakout rooms within Zoom and we had a volunteer there to help you as needed.

StPaulTim (Mar 18 2021 at 22:33):

For those who are interested, our plans are to hold Backdrop LIVE events every release cycle (3 times per year), the next one is planned for July 10th (approximately). There was some feedback that this might be too frequent and/or that July 10th is in the midst of vacation season.

We discussed this during the Outreach meeting today and the overall sentiment seems to be that we should stick with 3 events per year that are smaller and less formal and that given other scheduling issues, we might as well stick with July 10th for this year, recognizing the conflicts at that time of year. Part of the reason for doing 3 per year, is to provide enough opportunities for participation that no one needs to feel too bad about missing one. We expect that one event per year will be bigger than the others and that one is likely to take place in the fall.
