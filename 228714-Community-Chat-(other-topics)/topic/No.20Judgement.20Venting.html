Phil Ward (Nov 30 2020 at 08:46):

**Rant
One of the major things I hate about Open Source, is that a lot of people want something added or changed, but the people who have the permission to make the changes, won't make up their mind and just get it done.

Years of going back and forth, tweaking something until everyone is blue in the face, only to watch it sit there with no one willing to make the call.

"Just pick a direction and go! If it doesn't work, it doesn't work! Fix it and move on!"

Thinking about an issue is good, but overthinking tends to be at the core of a lot of open source projects.
**End Rant

Disclaimer: This is not directed at the backdrop community or anyone within it, but rather my experiences over the last two decades of watching project issues sit idle for years with no resolution. Schema.org specifically, brought up these feelings if you were wondering.
