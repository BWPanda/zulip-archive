What can we do to make Backdrop CMS more attractive to Drupal 7 site owners and developers?

oadaeh (Jason Flatt) (Mar 12 2021 at 17:39):

And known.

bugfolder (Mar 12 2021 at 17:39):

My number-1 answer: make things more discoverable from within the UI. I learned several things during Tim's Layout session that I never would have discovered on my own .

bugfolder (Mar 12 2021 at 17:40):

My initial idea is to sprinkle "additional information" popups liberally through the UI: a ? in a circle icon, that when you click on it, brings up a short bit of information about the topic, plus one or more links to exactly that area of the documentation.

bugfolder (Mar 12 2021 at 17:41):

That's something that even people who are non-experts in development could contribute via PRs; you don't have to know the intricacies of how layouts do their thing, you just need to know useful bits that people might want to know (and how to put it into a tooltip and file a PR).

oadaeh (Jason Flatt) (Mar 12 2021 at 17:41):

I did have a D5 site upgrade cross my path in the last year, but I think they moved on because their previous site builder did not think Backdrop would be good for them, based on their needs. But I think D9 is majorly overkill for their site, and will be way more expensive then they really can afford to pay.

oadaeh (Jason Flatt) (Mar 12 2021 at 17:42):

So there's a case of someone in the know recommending a solution that is inappropriate, possibly because of perception, but I'm not sure.

bugfolder (Mar 12 2021 at 17:45):

The basic idea being, when someone is exploring the UI and encounters an unfamiliar term for the first time ('like "flexible layout template"), there's something right there they can click on to learn more, rather than having to go leave the site and start Google-searching or paging through the docs to find it.

bugfolder (Mar 12 2021 at 17:46):

My number-2 answer is (which I am making contributions to) is get Coder Upgrade working more completely, and then emphasize it in all docs talking about migration. From personal experience, it was a HUGE help in porting D7 modules (even with the current bugs).

bugfolder (Mar 12 2021 at 17:47):

People may not realize how close Backdrop is to D7 (Layouts and Config notwithstanding), but they really don't know that there's a tool that does a lot of the work for them in porting their custom modules.

Phil Ward (Mar 12 2021 at 18:41):

I'm still in the boat of "get infront of those users". The BUS module is the single best way to show d7 users how close (or far) they are away from being able to move their site from D7 to Backdrop.

Just knowing about Backdrop isn't enough. Showing some of the differences isn't enough. Showing them "your site is 90% ready to be ported" is what that userbase is going to be the most interested in.

I speak from experience. I could have cared less about Backdrop as an option until I found out that Ubercart was ported. That's what got me excited. Then I ran into the wall of realizing some of the powerhouse modules I need (for example entity reference) isn't available yet.

I'm now in a holding pattern of learn more but not looking to port anytime soon.

Point is, if we aren't able to show D7 users a clear and viable path to move from D7 to Backdrop, BD is nothing more than a novelty idea (to those users) that works but isn't polished enough to consider for mainstream porting. Maybe it's good enough as a small basic site to get the feet wet or good to start a brand new site on for moving forward, but if we can't prove to them it is good enough to port a tenured site, they are going to continue to weigh their options with more established projects and keep Backdrop in the far back of their minds.

docwilmot (Mar 12 2021 at 18:45):

@Phil Ward still question though: do they know Backdrop exists? They'll only get to BUS module if the answer is yes.

Phil Ward (Mar 12 2021 at 20:02):

@docwilmot Agreed.

However I don't feel like it's a good idea to get the BUS module in front of people if they're going to see say 30% of their site is ready for being ported. If we can get that closer to 65% or 75% for most sites, people will be much more willing to look closely at Backdrop as an option. This is why I would like to see the telemetry added to BUS, so we can see where to address focus and get more D7 sites to a 65 - 75% portability status.

But yes, we need to get BUS and Backdrop in front of more Drupal folks as a viable option.
