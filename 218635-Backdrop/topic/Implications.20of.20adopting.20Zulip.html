BWPanda (Jan 05 2020 at 19:10):

@StPaulTim True, but it might be like when Backdrop had to cull their mailing list - it's really only those who aren't/haven't been active that we'll be 'losing'. And even then, if they ever do check in on Gitter there'll be a message about having moved official support, etc. here, and I'm sure there'll still be people (like myself) who'll be willing to check Gitter regularly and continue to provide 'unofficial' support there.

I wonder if using Zulip would have a positive or negative effect on NEW users joining the chat community.

BWPanda (Jan 05 2020 at 19:28):

Laryn (Jan 06 2020 at 23:00):

The docs seem to refer to a "guest" role/permission but I'm not 100% sure how that role works (it may still require registration and log in):
https://zulipchat.com/help/stream-permissions

But if Zulip becomes the live chat and people are encouraged to post/summarize good topics in the (public) forum, it may be okay that it's not all immediately browsable without registering.

Stream privacy settings (Zulip Help Center)
Streams are similar to chatrooms, IRC channels, or email lists in that they determine who receives a message. There are three types of streams in Zulip. | Public: Anyone other than guests can join, and anyone (other than guests) can view the complete message history without joining. | Private, shared history: You must be added by a member of the stream. The complete message history is available as soon as you are added. | Private, protected history: You must be added by a member of the stream. You only have access to the messages that are sent after you are added.
