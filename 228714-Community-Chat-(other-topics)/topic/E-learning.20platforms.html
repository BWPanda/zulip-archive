ericfg (Mar 24 2020 at 15:39):

and FYI: on my covid days to-do list is a small module to allow for integration between moodle and backdropcms (starting from the sorta working moodle connector module for drupal7

indigoxela (Mar 24 2020 at 16:03):

That's interesting, what's the benefit of an integration? In other words: what are the roles for both, Moodle and Backdrop in your concept?

ericfg (Mar 24 2020 at 16:10):

in the system in question, all courses are recored long-term in drupal/civicrm (hopefully soon to move to backdrop/civicrm). moodle gets refreshed often due to various organizational reasons.
so integration is:
1: being able to register students in Baz la (what we call the civi/drupal tool in this organization); class is setup in moodle, class id is entered in bazla, students automatically registered for the correct classes in moodle
2: exposing moodle class information to views so students when they log into bazla see a block with relevant info (upcoming sessions, things that are due, links to the moodle classes they are in)
3: possibly pulling final class grades into bazla from moodle

ericfg (Mar 24 2020 at 16:11):

so transcripts/long term records for reporting, etc are all stored in backdrop (drupal7) and civicrm . but moodle can be used for day to day classes

indigoxela (Mar 24 2020 at 16:14):

Fancy...

ericfg (Mar 24 2020 at 16:15):

one goal was to detach LMS from the tool that is used for everything long term. So they could in theory swap out moodle for sakai if the teachers wanted to. allow for things to move at different speeds for the services that are delivered to students which might change rapidly, and the tool for administrative use which needs to stay consistent

ericfg (Mar 24 2020 at 16:18):

one of the things i like most about moodle, is that I never ever have to fight with it. somehow, it has just worked out of the box for everything they've needed so far. I'm sure that's because the teachers are willing to live with problems and work around them without complaining (a social reality of the project that I have tried to change)
