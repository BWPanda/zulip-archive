At the outreach meeting, we talked about how to get more people trying BackDrop. The thought at the time was that it's the developers who need to try it out because businesses should have a tech person, a web person, or internal developer type, who does the web site for the business. So the focus is attracting existing web developers, like Drupal 7 developers.

My thought is that there are millions of people out of work and millions of small businesses that don't have a web presence. Many people have lost their jobs (about 50 million) and are needing to create some kind for work for themselves. These could be massage therapists, authors, woodworkers, painters, repair in various industries, etc. They are mom and pop businesses and they don't have a tech and don't have money to hire professional developers. I was once quoted $4000 to upgrade my site. New home businesses can't spend money like that. But they need to be on the web, otherwise they are invisible.

We need to make BackDrop even more friendly for very small businesses and non-profits to create a web site. I suggest that BackDrop Live could explore how we could help these millions of people create work for themselves and create a web presence to promote themselves. I have one of these small businesses myself and it took me years to create my Drupal 7 sites. It was hard. My wife also is starting a small business. She tried to become a front end developer and spent two years studying. She finally gave up. Too difficult, to much complexity.

Now she is starting a machine embroidery design business. For a web presence she is using WordPress, because it is easy to get installed and use. Media, Photos and Video are ready to go at the install. BackDrop should be the easiest and also the most powerful CMS for these millions of people. We need to make the documentation and process very straightforward. Step by step. Everything should be ready so that installation is a breeze and putting up pages with photos and YouTube videos is ready to go.

Just some thoughts to consider.
I'm working on "The Ultimate Guide to BackDrop for Beginners" that will start this process going.
I'm just documenting all my trials and tribulations to find all the weak points in the process of creating a BackDrop web site.
