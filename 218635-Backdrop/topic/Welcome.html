StPaulTim (Jan 05 2020 at 18:47):

It's my understanding that replies are always in chronological order. I believe, that if you reply to another message that means that your reply will have the same topic, but it will still appear as the most recent post in the stream and in the topic.

Zulip seems to give the impression that it allows threads. But, in fact, it's really a single stream of posts in chronological order - but, with the ability to filter by stream or topic. When you reply to a post, you have ability to control which stream and topic your post belongs to. But, I think, your post will always appear as the most recent post in either the respective stream and/or topic.

BWPanda (Jan 05 2020 at 18:51):

I concur with @StPaulTim (from what I've seen and understand of Zulip). Think of replying to someone in Zulip like replying to them in Gitter. Your reply will still appear at the end of the list and will not really have any connection to the post you're replying to (other than maybe the @username tag), but Zulip has the added ability to hide all other posts in the channel not related to that topic, so makes it a bit easier to follow a related conversation.

BWPanda (Jan 06 2020 at 08:17):

It seems that we can't easily link to repos with a . in the name (e.g. github.com/backdrop-ops/backdropcms.org) as it's not a supported character in the regex where this is setup. I've emailed Zulip support about this.

BWPanda (Jan 06 2020 at 08:20):

To clarify, you can type a hash (#) followed by a number and it'll automatically link to that issue in the main Backdrop issue queue. To link to a different issue queue, use the syntax: org/repo#number where org is the GitHub organisation (e.g. 'backdrop', 'backdrop-ops', 'backdrop-contrib'), repo is the repository name and number is the issue number to link to.

BWPanda (Jan 06 2020 at 08:49):

Linking to Pull Requests works similarly: #pr[number] (e.g. #pr123)
And: [org]/[repo]#pr[number] (e.g. backdrop-ops/contrib#pr352)
